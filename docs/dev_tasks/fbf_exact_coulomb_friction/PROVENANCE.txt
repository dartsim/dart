2. Paper implementation availability
-------------------------------------

Historical observation: on 2026-07-12 the official project page still said

the MIT-licensed repository was public and was pinned for this task at commit
b3f3c5ca646b39a1bc4fbd8c3ebfb6810fee4bd0. It contains the Warp/Newton FBF
solver, six runnable paper-example scene/configuration sources, a pinned

It does not contain the historical paper renderer cameras/materials/goldens,

DART scenes created before that release remain reconstructions until each is
audited against the pinned source and matched numerically. They must not be
described as byte-identical author scenes merely because the source is now
available.

2a. Paper and current-source gamma policy
-----------------------------------------

The paper publishes the safe spectral base step, the local coupling-variation
acceptance threshold 0.9, and the 0.7 shrink applied when that test rejects a
trial. The paper text does not publish a cross-step recovery/growth rule, but
the pinned public reference now exposes its current `GammaController` carry,
growth, shrink, and warm-start-cap policy. DART's persisted-gamma recovery,
including its current 1.05 growth factor and fresh safe-bound cap, remains a
local implementation policy until a source-equivalence audit proves otherwise.
The current source and historical paper-text distinctions must both remain
explicit.

5. DART geometry port
---------------------

Implementation: dart/math/detail/MasonryArchGeometry.hpp

The port reproduces the weighted-catenary generator parameters fc=60 cm,
Qb=100 cm^2, Qt=49 cm^2, and L=30 cm, converts centimeters to meters, and
retains base flattening. It exposes two distinct fidelity tiers: the existing
oriented-box approximation used by the dynamic DART scenes, and literal
eight-vertex wedge collision geometry. The wedge generator can either retain
Rigid-IPC's alternating 0.1 cm IPC barrier offsets or omit them so adjacent
end faces share their nominal plane. Neither tier is a byte-identical FBF
author scene. In particular, the dynamic box scenes must not be called
author-faithful or described as literal tapered voussoirs.

The literal wedge generator supplies exact uniform-prism centroid, volume,
mass, and inertia rather than ConvexMeshShape's AABB approximation. The
collision probe remains collision-only. A distinct, explicitly non-paper
`masonry_arch_25_literal_wedge` trace now uses the same exact-inertia geometry
for a numeric dynamic reconstruction without replacing the production
oriented-box scenes. Its standing, solver, and timing evidence is scoped in
section 7a; it supplies no author-scene, crown-impact, or GUI claim.

6a. Card-house reconstruction
------------------------------

The paper publishes 26 plates across four levels, mu = 0.8, dt = 1/60 s,
settling through 6.7 s, and four projectiles before the 10 s view. It does not
publish plate dimensions, mass or inertia, A-frame angle, level spacing,
initial penetration, projectile parameters, collision margin, or manifold
reduction policy. The current DART card scene therefore remains a
reconstruction and is never an author-scene fixture.

Current reconstruction values are 1.0 x 0.45 x 0.03 m plates, 0.05 kg per
plate, a 0.23 rad A-frame angle, and a nominal 0.003 m interface overlap.
A-frame centers are derived from the oriented boxes' horizontal half extents
rather than a fitted contact-count constant. Horizontal one-meter supports are
spaced by 0.997 m so adjacent supports have the same nominal 0.003 m overlap.
The earlier 0.96-half-span and 0.55 m-spacing heuristics were removed because
they created approximately 0.037 m apex penetration and 0.45 m coplanar
support overlap, respectively.

Actual contacts, unique body pairs, penetration statistics, residuals, and
timings must be measured for every run. Neither detector caps nor geometry may
be tuned merely until a row equals the paper's reported contact count.

7. Arch simulation parameters and boundary-condition distinction
------------------------------------------------------------------

Rigid-IPC source scene values used by the current DART geometry route:

- coefficient_friction = 0.5, uniform;
- density = 1000 kg/m^3 when unspecified by the source scene;
- gravity magnitude = 9.8 m/s^2 in the source JSON;
- all stones dynamic and only the ground fixed.

Published FBF evidence is a separate contract:

- contact-rich tables and gamma sweeps report mu = 0.8;
- the 25-stone paper scene describes two pinned end stones and 23 dynamic
  interior stones;
- the paper timing row reports 100 contacts for 25 stones;
- the paper text does not publish all projectile, initialization, collision,
  or stabilization parameters; and
- the pinned public author source now supplies current masonry-arch meshes,
  scene defaults, projectile/drop controls, solver configuration, and timeline,
  but equivalence to the historical table run still requires an explicit
  audit.

Accordingly, current DART runs using mu=0.5 and all-dynamic Rigid-IPC
boundaries are geometry-source experiments, not paper-timing parity. A
paper-contract arch run must set mu=0.8, pin the two springers, record its
actual contact count, stabilization policy, residual distribution, physical
outcome, and zero-fallback status, and retain these differences in its capture
sidecar/performance metadata.

The production dynamic scenes use source-derived weighted-catenary oriented
boxes, not literal wedges. The 25-stone production construction measures 96
natural box-manifold contacts before its GUI reduction to 48; the 101-stone
full construction reaches its 512-contact cap, so its uncapped natural count
is unknown (the GUI profile reduces to 38).

The separate literal 25-wedge collision audit applies mu=0.8, pins springers 0
and 24, uses exact uniform-prism mass properties, and does not step dynamics.
Across the audited Native/FCL/Bullet configurations it yields 24 nominal
adjacent pairs; adding the audit's bounded closure and ground yields 26. No
audited backend reaches the paper timing row's 100-contact 25-stone contract.
The collision-probe metadata therefore remains
exact_polyhedral_inertia=true, collision_only=true, and dynamic_claim=false.

8. Integrity rule
------------------

Only artifacts listed in paper-evidence-manifest.json with existing paths and
validated metadata count as evidence. Malformed, interrupted, concatenated,
fallback-contaminated, or unverifiable runs remain explicitly invalid or
partial and must never be used for completion or performance claims. Timing
and real-time claims additionally require a validated physical outcome and a
controlled affinity contract. Pre-hardening visual artifacts lack the
every-step/all-group diagnostic contract and are stale until recaptured.

The validator hashes repository artifacts, materializes the CPU,
standing-visual, incline, and prior-source indexes, and binds recorded process results,
taskset commands, per-CPU topology aggregates, worker-residency identities,
and archived-prior-source provenance without requiring ignored local build
products. It independently recomputes the CPU timing, thread-residency,
colored-dispatch, physical, and solver-option claims from the 3,600-row scale
bundle and 5,220-row current-small bundle, then cross-checks the card-v2-r3,
impact-v8, and arch101-v6 semantic
fields and promotion boundaries.

The finalized Painleve proxy bundle is independently validated as an exact
27-artifact membership set in a 29-file physical directory. Validation
recomputes every indexed byte size and
hash; binds source/test/binary identities; checks both member sidecars, media
streams, paired composition, full decode, manual panel verdicts, and separate
151-row tracked traces; and recomputes the first-tumble and shorter pre-tumble
contracts. Paper/external/golden/timing/real-time promotion and rendered-demo
to tracked-trace equivalence remain explicitly false or null.

The finalized incline bundle is independently validated as an exact
21-artifact membership set in a 23-file physical directory. Validation
recomputes indexed sizes and hashes, binds source/test/binary/runtime
identities, checks five durable stills and the decoded clip/panel/manual
